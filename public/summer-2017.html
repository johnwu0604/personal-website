Pushing Beyond My Boundaries.

Preamble - A Journey Awaits

April 29, 2017

Hey There! Thanks for checking out my summer 2017 blog page! For those who don't already know, I was selected this year to be part the Cansbridge Fellowship. In summary, the fellowship is a program which aims to foster young entrepreneurs by immersing them into challenging environments, connecting them to a supportive network, and accelerating their growth to realize their aspirations. As a result, this will lead to quite an exciting summer ahead of me and I'll be using this page to document my journey. My summer will begin in May at the Cansbridge Fellowship Conference (CFC) in the San Francisco Bay Area. This will be the kick-off to my journey and a chance to explore the dream destination of every tech entrepreneur. Following the conference, I'll be living in Tokyo, Japan and working for a startup called Flickfit for three months. Going into a completely foreign country, I'll be pushing myself outside of my comfort zone and be challenged to rebuild my support network from scratch. Upon finishing my internship, I'll be travelling through Asia for about a month and visiting as many different countries as possible. This will be my final chance to truly push my limits and to learn as much as possible while being abroad. I'm hoping to post bi-weekly updates about my experience here, so stay tuned for my first article!



Chapter 1 - Silicon Valley

May 14, 2017

In May of 2010, coming fresh out of my first high school basketball season, I had showcased my skills enough to be selected for the local club team (Athelite Basketball). This was a unique chance to be part of an elite team that would spend the summer competing at various tournaments in order to continue our development during the off-season. Our first tournament was in Seattle, and for most of us, our first taste at American level competition. Upon walking into the location of our first game, we were amazed: a facility split into 10+ games going on simultaneously, polished floors without a hint of dust, and coolers full of free gatorade for all the players. Once the game had ended, we were stunned: bruises on every part of our body, completely out of breath, and a 50+ point loss. We were some of the better players north of the border, but this kept us humble yet motivated us to take our game to the next level.

7 years later now as a tech geek and aspiring entrepreneur, I managed to experience some parallel comparisons when visiting the silicon valley for the Cansbridge Fellowship Conference. Uncomparable working environments, outstanding salaries, and sunny California weather were some of the perks I got to witness, but the fierce competition and standards to be able to make it to the valley did not go unnoticed. Myself along with the other Cansbridge Cohort have had accomplishments within Canada, but witnessing the ecosystem in the valley has inspired all of us to go beyond the Canadian market to compete at a global scale.

My 10 day stay in San Francisco was definitely an eye-opening experience and a teaser to how much more I can experience by leaving the comfort zone that is in Canada. The week consisted of touring offices and exploring the bay area while working on a startup pitch that was presented to Boris Wertz (one of the top tech angel investors in North-America). However, the best and most important part of the week was getting to know the Cansbridge Fellowship Network which consists of not just some of the top minds through Canada, but also some of the most genuine people who I have ever met. I got to learn about how the program began in Queen's University just six years ago, has since grown into a nation-wide network of leaders, and has the potential to continue growing for years to come. After experiencing more of what Cansbridge truly is, I feel extremely lucky to be part of this fellowship and I look forward to where my life will take me as a result!

Next up, the Cansbridge 2017 Cohort will be spending the summer working in various parts of Asia. I'll be working in Tokyo, so if anyone is in the area, feel free to reach out to me! And if you are one of the cohorts reading this, good luck with your journey :)
